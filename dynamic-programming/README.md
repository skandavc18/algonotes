# Dynamic Programming Series

The most popular articles on our public account are from the dynamic-programming series — perhaps because DP problems are challenging and interesting, perhaps because they are frequently asked in interviews. Whether or not you've previously been intimidated by DP problems, the content of this chapter should be enough to help you get over your fear of dynamic programming.

Specifically, the general DP workflow is three steps: **brute-force recursive solution -> recursive solution with a memo -> iterative DP solution**.

In terms of the thought process, it breaks down into a few steps: **identify the state and the choices -> clearly define the dp array/function -> find the relationship between states**.

This is the framework of the mindset. **Throughout this chapter we'll solve problems following the pattern above, helping the reader develop this kind of pattern-based thinking**. Once you have a direction, you won't be lost when facing a problem, and this is enough to solve most DP problems.
